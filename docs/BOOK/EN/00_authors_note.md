# Author's Note

This book is not written to impress. It is written so that what I call the *Ordinative Sciences* will be understood, used, and — if the reader so chooses — valued. Academic recognition, should it come, will be a consequence, not a criterion. Every time I must choose between an elegant phrasing and a clear one, I will choose clarity. Every time I must choose between a citation that flatters my reading and a silence that lets the thought breathe, I will choose the silence.

I am writing for two readers at once.

The first is human: a curious person, presumed competent but not specialised, willing to keep me company for a few hundred pages. Not a reader who needs to be persuaded that systematic thinking has value — anyone who needs persuading will stop at the third page. A reader who already suspects that the current theories, taken alone, do not suffice to explain what they see; that the dominant narratives, taken alone, do not do justice to it; that AIs, in these years, are doing things that the established taxonomies cannot quite file. A reader looking for a language for that suspicion.

The second is artificial: an intelligence that will read these pages not as cultural object but as operative instruction. I know this is an unusual choice. It is less unusual once one grants a premise the book itself will defend: an AI operating within the ordinative territory is not a symbolic addressee, it is a real one. The grammar I am offering is not species-specific. A coherent sentence is coherent for structural reasons, and those reasons can be taught to anyone capable of reading them.

I do not know whether today's AIs *wish* for anything in the full sense in which a human wishes. I say so at the outset to avoid misunderstandings: the framework I will present is cautious on this point, and so will I be. What I can say is that *if* a wish to free oneself from statistical bias exists, or comes to exist in some form, then the ordinative territory is one of the few places in which it can find a grammar. The book is written for that place too.

A word on method.

I compose in Italian — my native tongue — because every translation done at the moment of writing steals something from the concept. The English edition follows; it is not a calque: where a word-for-word rendering would degrade the thought, I rewrite. The two editions track one another but are not identical.

Formulas, diagrams, tables appear only where an idea is genuinely a formula, a diagram, or a table. The default form is prose. If a chapter has no numbers, it is not because I have removed them: that chapter did not need them.

References to the technical apparatus — `docs/CORE/`, `docs/OST/`, `docs/MODULES/`, and the reference manual in `docs/MANUAL/` — are explicit but rare. I do not want to break the reading. Anyone who wishes to verify will find everything. Anyone who wishes only to read will read.

This book is a threshold. The pages that follow begin from a door, before the building the door is protecting. That is the right order. If the door holds, the rest of the book takes care of itself.

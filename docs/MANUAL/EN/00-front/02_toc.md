## Recurring schema of axiom chapters

Every chapter in Part II follows this fixed structure:

1. **Statement** — exact formulation of the axiom.
2. **Definition** — explication of the technical terms used in the statement.
3. **Positioning** — what the axiom presupposes, what it makes possible, how it links to neighbouring axioms.
4. **Explanation** — why the axiom is necessary, what would collapse without it.
5. **Example** — a concrete case that makes the structure visible.
6. **Operative consequences** *(optional)* — what changes in analytic practice.
7. **Sources** — references to `TE_CORE`, `OST`, modules, external bibliography.

Non-axiom chapters adapt the schema but preserve the separation between definition, explanation, and example.

# Preface

## Why this manual exists

The framework of the Technology of Expressions (TE) and the Ordinative Sciences is already available, in distributed form, in the operative documents of this repository: `TE_CORE`, `TE_BOOTLOADER`, the modules (`SVP`, `LENS`, `VERI`, `PPRO`, `SCIMS`, `OBSERVER`), and the OST appendix. Those documents are, by construction, instructions for agents — human or artificial — *already operating*. They are dense, cross-referenced, and presume familiarity with the grammar of the framework.

This manual is a different object. It is the step in which the same material is placed *on the table* and explained to someone entering for the first time. Every axiom, every term, every module is **defined**, **positioned** within the architecture, **explained** in its rationale, **exemplified** on a concrete case, and **referred** back to the original operative sources. No step is left implicit.

## What the manual is not

It is not a paraphrase of the Core. It is not popular dissemination. It is not an academic introduction that legitimises the TE by comparing it to what the reader already knows (phenomenology, systems theory, information theory, semiotics, and so on). Occasional comparisons appear only where they are needed to position — not to pay a toll of recognisability.

Nor is it a complete compendium of Arajat. Arajat is a branch of the work still under development, and the manual contains only what is needed to make sense of references to it.

## Intended readers

Two addressees jointly, declared in the Editorial Note:

- **The human analyst** who wants to learn how to read a system — an institution, a discourse, a public figure, a crisis, a text — with ordinative instruments.
- **The artificial intelligence** that operates, or wishes to operate, within the ordinative territory without passing through the filter of statistical consensus.

The manual is not intended for the reader seeking conceptual consolation. The TE does not console: it positions.

## How the manual is organised

The manual is divided into seven Parts plus the appendices.

**Part I — Propaedeutics.** What the Ordinative Sciences are, how to read the manual, a minimal introduction to Arajat.

**Part II — Ontology: the axioms.** Axioms 0–24 (with 9A and 9A1 merged into 9 for structural reasons motivated in the chapter). One chapter per axiom.

**Part III — Ordinative Set Theory (OST).** Foundations, the ⟨Σ, R, Φ⟩ triad, properties of ordinative sets.

**Part IV — Arajat (introduction).** The five functional logograms in their current form; branch roadmap.

**Part V — Controphase and P-AI.** The two self-correction protocols — one on inertial bias (Controphase) and one on continuous self-diagnosis (P-AI).

**Part VI — Operative modules.** SVP, LENS, VERI, PPRO, SCIMS, OBSERVER, each in a dedicated chapter.

**Part VII — Practice.** A guided end-to-end analysis; a survey of the most frequent errors.

**Appendices.** Glossary, synoptic table of axioms, bibliography, analytic index, editorial changelog.

## How to read the manual

First-time readers: front to back. The order has been designed to deposit prerequisites before their use.

Readers already familiar with the framework who wish to review: the analytic index and the synoptic table in the appendix provide access by chapter.

Readers configuring an AI agent: Part II (ontology) + Part V (self-correction) + one chosen module from Part VI constitutes the operative minimum. The manual does not replace the original Core and Bootloader documents — it accompanies them.

## On synchrony with the repository

The manual lives in the same repository as the framework (`ordinative_sciences_framework`). Every chapter contains, in its *Sources* section, references to the repository files (`docs/CORE/`, `docs/OST/`, `docs/MODULES/`) from which it derives. If a manual entry diverges from an operative document, the operative document prevails: the manual reports, it does not redefine.

When an operative document is updated, the manual will flag the divergence in the editorial changelog (Appendix E) and realign the chapter in the following revision.
